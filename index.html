The end of the road

  • First Team
  • March 7, 2018 3:30 AM
  • Share

Despite a magnificent atmosphere in the Parc des Princes, PSG bows out of the UEFA Champions League at the last-16 stage, going down 2-1 to Real Madrid. Brutal...

THE MATCH: PARIS COME UP SHORT…

The evening started in a passionate, festive atmosphere in a volcanic, sold out Parc des Princes, but ended, 94 minutes later in tatters. Like in the first leg, Paris were overrun in the second half at the hands of the twice defending European champions…

The 22 players started the match at a cracking tempo, as the high-intensity environment wrestled away in a tactical battle. Paris enjoyed slightly more of the ball in the opening half, but the Spanish side remained dangerous on the counterattack. It was Madrid that had the first chance of the night when Areola produced a great save to deny Ramos from a corner (18’)!

Areola kept his side in it but…,

Mbappé’s curling effort almost snuck inside the far post (29’) before Areola kept the tie alive with a brilliant save as Benzema raced clear one-on-one (38’)! With half-time looming, Paris went on the attack in search of that crucial opening goal. In 60 seconds, Di Maria (41’) and then Mbappé (42’) both tested Navas. Scoreless at the break, nothing had changed: Paris (who took three shots in the first half) still needed two unanswered goals. But that equation took a nasty turn for Les Parisiens shortly after the restart when Ronaldo headed home from close range (0-1, 51’)… And the Parc suffered another blow just 15 minutes later when Verratti was sent off for a second yellow card (66’). Nothing was going right for Les Rouge et Bleu…

By Edouard Chevalie
